Dig responsive design? Hate fluid grids? Try a Frameless grid.

Building a Frameless grid

1. Make a fixed-width grid.

Pick a column width, a gutter width, and other dimensions — you know, the usual stuff. Use whatever criteria it is that you usually use to create grids.

2. Center it in the viewport.

Align your grid to the middle of your viewport. For a symmetric grid (left), align the center point of your viewport in the middle of the gutter between your centermost columns. For an asymmetric grid (right), align it in the middle of your centermost column.

3. Make it infinite.

Now, make your grid repeat endlessly. Imagine that no matter how wide you make your viewport, more and more columns come into view from the sides. Think of bees. Imagine you're looking at an infinitely wide honeycomb.

4. That's it, really. Start using it.

Adapt your design column by column, using media queries. Or don't. You can choose exactly when your layout should adapt. This site, for example, only adapts around 320, 480, 600, 912 and 1900 px.

Tips & FAQs

Is Frameless responsive?

"Responsive web design“, as defined by its coiner, Ethan Marcotte, consists of three components: a fluid grid, fluid media, and media queries. The Frameless grid is fixed-width, not fluid.

Is Frameless a framework?

Nope. It's just a type of a grid that works as a nice starting point for an adaptive design. You still have to do all the hard work yourself.

What about Less Framework?

Frameless is sort of the spiritual successor to Less Framework. It's a much simpler, more flexible, and hopefully easier to integrate into your design workflow.

Don't adapt unless it makes sense.

Only adapt your design when it makes sense for the content to adapt; you don't have to fill up every column at every possible screen size.

Design Mobile First.

Start from the smallest possible size and work your way upwards. Adaptive designs tend to just work out better that way. Trust me.

Serve IE6–8 just the mobile layout.

Old IE won't see any styles that are inside a media query, so just serve it your mobile layout and enhance it a little with IE-specific classes. Not having to worry about old IE makes coding the wider layouts so much easier. More about this in my blog post: Leaving Old Internet Explorer Behind.
